📘 Overview of HSE

A comprehensive overview of Health, Safety and Environment (HSE) principles, systems, and best practices for engineering and industrial projects.

🔹 What is HSE?

HSE stands for Health, Safety, and Environment. It is a discipline that focuses on protecting people, property, and the environment from harm caused by industrial and engineering activities. HSE encompasses occupational health, workplace safety, environmental protection, and risk management.

🔹 Key HSE Areas

  • Safety Management – Systems, policies, and procedures to prevent accidents and injuries
  • Environmental Management – Minimizing environmental impact, waste management, and sustainability
  • Occupational Health – Protecting worker health and wellbeing
  • Risk Assessment – Identifying hazards and implementing controls
  • Safety Culture – Building a proactive safety mindset across the organization

🔹 Key HSE Standards

  • ISO 45001 – Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
  • ISO 14001 – Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 31000 – Risk Management
  • IEC 61508 – Functional Safety
  • NFPA 70E – Electrical Safety in the Workplace
