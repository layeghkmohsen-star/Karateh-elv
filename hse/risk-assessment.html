⚠️ Risk Assessment

Methods, tools, and best practices for hazard identification, risk analysis, and risk evaluation in engineering and industrial environments.

🔹 What is Risk Assessment?

Risk assessment is a systematic process of identifying hazards, analyzing and evaluating the risks they pose, and determining appropriate control measures. It is a fundamental part of health, safety, and environmental management, helping organizations prevent accidents, injuries, and environmental damage.

🔹 Key Steps in Risk Assessment

  • Hazard Identification – Identify all potential hazards associated with activities, equipment, or materials
  • Risk Analysis – Determine the likelihood and consequence of each hazard
  • Risk Evaluation – Rank risks based on severity and decide on priorities
  • Risk Control – Select and implement measures to eliminate or reduce risks
  • Monitoring & Review – Regularly review risk assessments and update as conditions change

🔹 Common Risk Assessment Tools

  • Risk Matrix – A qualitative tool for assessing likelihood and consequence
  • HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study) – Structured analysis for process safety
  • FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis) – Identifying potential failure modes and their impacts
  • JSA (Job Safety Analysis) – Breaking down tasks to identify hazards
  • LOPA (Layer of Protection Analysis) – Evaluating layers of protection against hazards
  • What-If Analysis – Brainstorming potential scenarios

🔹 Example: Risk Assessment for a Chemical Storage Area

A risk assessment is conducted for a chemical storage area. The team identifies hazards: chemical spills, fire, exposure to toxic fumes, and falling objects. Using a risk matrix, they assess each risk and prioritize controls (e.g., bunding for spills, fire suppression systems, ventilation, PPE). The assessment is reviewed annually and updated after any changes to the storage area.
