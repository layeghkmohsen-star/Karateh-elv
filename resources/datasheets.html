🔹 What are Technical Datasheets?

A technical datasheet (or data sheet) is a document that summarizes the performance, technical characteristics, and specifications of a product, material, or component. Datasheets are essential for engineers during the design, selection, and procurement phases of a project. They provide the critical data needed to ensure compatibility, performance, and compliance with project requirements.

🔹 Key Information in a Datasheet

  • Product Overview – Name, type, and description of the product
  • Technical Specifications – Electrical ratings, mechanical dimensions, environmental conditions, and performance characteristics
  • Electrical Characteristics – Voltage, current, power, frequency, and impedance
  • Mechanical Characteristics – Dimensions, weight, mounting, and materials
  • Environmental Conditions – Temperature range, humidity, IP rating, and altitude
  • Compliance – Certifications (CE, UL, CSA, IEC) and standards met
  • Ordering Information – Part numbers, options, and accessories
  • Typical Applications – Examples of how the product is used
  • Safety Information – Warnings, precautions, and safety ratings

  • APIs and Aggregators – Octopart, FindChips

🔹 Example: Selecting a Motor Based on Datasheet

An engineer is selecting a 45 kW induction motor for a pump application. They review datasheets from different manufacturers to compare efficiency, starting torque, frame size, weight, and protection rating (IP55 vs IP65). Based on the datasheet data, the engineer selects a motor that meets the required performance, fits the space constraints, and provides the best efficiency.
