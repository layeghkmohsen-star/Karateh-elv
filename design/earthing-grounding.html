⚡ Earthing & Grounding

Principles, design, and installation of earthing and grounding systems for safety, protection, and electromagnetic compatibility.

🔹 Importance of Earthing

Earthing (or grounding) is a critical safety measure that provides a low-impedance path for fault currents to flow to the earth. It protects personnel from electric shock, prevents equipment damage, ensures proper operation of protection devices, and reduces electromagnetic interference.

🔹 Types of Earthing Systems

  • TN System – Neutral directly earthed, exposed conductive parts connected to neutral.
  • TT System – Neutral earthed, exposed conductive parts earthed separately.
  • IT System – Neutral isolated or earthed via high impedance, exposed parts earthed.
  • Protective Earthing – For equipment and enclosures.
  • Functional Earthing – For sensitive electronic equipment (e.g., PLC, instruments).

🔹 Key Components

  • Earth Electrodes – Rods, plates, or strips buried in the ground.
  • Earthing Conductors – Copper or galvanized steel connections.
  • Earth Bars – Main distribution point for earthing connections.
  • Measurement Points – For testing resistance.

🔹 Example: Substation Earthing Design

A substation earthing system includes a ring earth electrode buried around the perimeter, connected to all equipment. The design ensures that touch and step voltages are within safe limits. Resistance to earth is measured using a fall-of-potential method to verify compliance with IEEE 80.
