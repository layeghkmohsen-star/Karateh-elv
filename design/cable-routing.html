🔌 Cable Routing

Principles and practices for routing cables in industrial facilities, including cable trays, conduits, segregation, and installation standards.

🔹 What is Cable Routing?

Cable routing is the process of planning and installing the physical pathways for electrical and instrumentation cables in industrial facilities. Proper routing ensures safety, minimizes interference, provides access for maintenance, and protects cables from mechanical and environmental damage.

🔹 Key Considerations

  • Segregation – Separate power and control cables to avoid interference
  • Supports – Use cable trays, conduits, or ladder racks with proper spacing
  • Bend Radius – Maintain minimum bend radius to prevent cable damage
  • Identification – Use tags, labels, and color coding for easy identification
  • Earthing – Proper grounding of cable trays and armor
  • Fire Protection – Use fire-resistant cables and fire stops in penetrations

🔹 Cable Routing Systems

  • Cable Tray – Ladder, perforated, or solid bottom for open routing
  • Conduit – Rigid or flexible metallic/plastic tubes for protection
  • Trunking – Enclosed channels for indoor wiring
  • Underground Ducts – For outdoor and buried installations

🔹 Example: Cable Tray Installation

A cable tray system is installed in a chemical plant to support power and control cables. The tray is mounted on supports every 1.5 meters, with segregation using dividers or separate trays. Cables are labeled at both ends and at regular intervals for easy identification.
