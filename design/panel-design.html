📦 Panel Design

Design principles, layout considerations, component selection, and wiring for industrial electrical control panels.

🔹 What is Panel Design?

Panel design is the process of planning and creating electrical control panels that house circuit breakers, contactors, relays, PLCs, and other components. Good panel design ensures safe operation, easy maintenance, proper heat dissipation, and compliance with standards.

🔹 Key Components

  • Enclosure – Cabinet with IP rating (IP54, IP66) for protection
  • Main Switch – Isolates the panel from power supply
  • Circuit Breakers – Protection for incoming and outgoing circuits
  • Contactors & Relays – Switching and control
  • PLCs/Controllers – The brains of the automation
  • Terminal Blocks – For wiring connections

🔹 Key Standards

  • IEC 61439 – Low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies
  • IEC 60529 – IP Ratings (Ingress Protection)
  • UL 508A – Industrial Control Panels (US)
  • IEC 60204 – Safety of machinery – Electrical equipment

🔹 Example: Motor Control Panel

A motor control panel includes an incoming circuit breaker, contactor, overload relay, and local start/stop buttons. The panel also includes a PLC for remote control and status monitoring. Layout ensures proper spacing for heat dissipation and easy access for maintenance.
