⚡ Power Distribution

Design and planning of power distribution systems, including load centers, switchgear, feeders, and protection coordination for industrial facilities.

🔹 What is Power Distribution?

Power distribution is the process of transferring electrical power from the main supply (grid, generators) to end-use loads such as motors, lighting, and equipment. It involves transformers, switchgear, distribution panels, feeders, and protection devices. Proper distribution ensures reliable, safe, and efficient operation.

🔹 Key Components

  • Main Switchgear – Receives power from utility or generators and distributes to loads.
  • Transformers – Step down voltage from medium to low voltage (e.g., 22 kV to 400 V).
  • Distribution Panels – Distribute power to local loads (motors, lighting, etc.).
  • Busbars – Conductors that carry current in switchgear and panels.
  • Feeders – Cables carrying power from switchgear to loads.
  • Protection Devices – Circuit breakers, fuses, relays for fault protection.

🔹 Distribution Configurations

  • Radial System – Simple, cost-effective, but single point of failure.
  • Ring Main System – Redundant, used in critical facilities.
  • Selective System – Segregates loads, improves reliability.
  • Spot Network – Multiple sources for high reliability.

🔹 Example: Industrial Plant Distribution

An industrial plant receives 22 kV power from the utility. A main transformer steps down to 400 V. The 400 V switchgear feeds several distribution panels in different areas of the plant. Each panel supplies motor control centers (MCCs), lighting panels, and other loads. Protection coordination ensures selective tripping for faults.
