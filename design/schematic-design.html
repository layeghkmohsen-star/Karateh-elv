📐 Schematic Design

Understanding and creating electrical schematic diagrams, including single-line, three-line, and wiring diagrams for industrial systems.

🔹 What is Schematic Design?

Schematic design is the process of creating symbolic representations of electrical circuits and systems. These diagrams use standardized symbols to show connections, components, and power distribution. They are essential for design, installation, troubleshooting, and maintenance of electrical systems.

🔹 Types of Schematics

  • Single-Line Diagram – Simplified representation of power distribution, showing main components and connections in one line.
  • Three-Line Diagram – Detailed representation showing all three phases of a power system with protective devices.
  • Wiring Diagram – Shows actual wiring connections between components for installation.
  • Control Schematic – Shows control logic, relays, contactors, and PLC I/O connections.

🔹 Standard Symbols

  • IEC 60617 – Standard electrical symbols
  • ANSI Y32.2 – American standard symbols
  • ISA 5.1 – Instrumentation symbols
  • Common symbols: switches, relays, motors, transformers, circuit breakers

🔹 Example: Motor Control Schematic

A motor control schematic includes a circuit breaker, contactor, overload relay, start/stop push buttons, and motor. The schematic shows the power circuit (supply to motor) and control circuit (push buttons and relay contacts) clearly using standard symbols.
