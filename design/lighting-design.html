💡 Lighting Design

Principles of lighting design for industrial and commercial facilities, including illuminance calculations, luminaire selection, and energy efficiency.

🔹 Importance of Lighting Design

Proper lighting design is essential for safety, productivity, and comfort in industrial and commercial spaces. Good lighting reduces accidents, improves visibility, enhances worker performance, and contributes to energy efficiency. Lighting design must meet standards for illuminance levels and uniformity.

🔹 Key Concepts

  • Illuminance (Lux) – The amount of light falling on a surface (lm/m²).
  • Luminous Flux (Lumens) – The total amount of visible light emitted by a source.
  • Color Temperature (K) – The color appearance of light (warm 2700K, cool 6500K).
  • Color Rendering Index (CRI) – How accurately colors are rendered.
  • Uniformity Ratio – The ratio of minimum to average illuminance.

🔹 Common Lighting Types

  • LED Lighting – High efficiency, long life, instant start, excellent color rendering.
  • Fluorescent Lighting – Good efficiency, but contains mercury.
  • HID (High-Intensity Discharge) – Metal halide, high-pressure sodium, used for outdoor and high-bay.
  • Emergency Lighting – Battery-backed, for safety during power outages.

🔹 Example: Warehouse Lighting Design

A warehouse requires an average illuminance of 300 lux at floor level. Using LED high-bay luminaires with 150 lm/W efficiency, the lighting designer calculates the number of fixtures required based on mounting height and spacing. The design ensures uniform illumination and minimizes shadows.
