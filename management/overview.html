📘 Overview of Engineering Management

A comprehensive overview of engineering management principles, including project management, risk, workflow, resources, cost, quality, and leadership.

🔹 What is Engineering Management?

Engineering management is the art and science of planning, organizing, directing, and controlling engineering activities and resources. It combines technical knowledge with management skills to deliver successful engineering projects on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

🔹 Key Management Areas

  • Project Management – Planning, scheduling, execution, and monitoring
  • Risk Management – Identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks
  • Engineering Workflow – Optimizing processes and handovers
  • Resource Planning – Managing people, equipment, and materials
  • Cost Estimation – Budgeting, cost control, and forecasting
  • Quality Management – Assurance, control, and continuous improvement
  • Leadership – Team building, communication, and decision-making

🔹 Key Standards and Frameworks

  • PMBOK – Project Management Body of Knowledge
  • ISO 21500 – Guidance on Project Management
  • ISO 31000 – Risk Management
  • ISO 9001 – Quality Management
  • Lean and Six Sigma – Process improvement
