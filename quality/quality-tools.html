🛠️ Quality Tools

The 7 basic quality control tools: Pareto charts, cause-and-effect diagrams, check sheets, histograms, control charts, scatter diagrams, and flowcharts.

🔹 The 7 QC Tools

The seven basic quality tools (7 QC tools) are a set of techniques widely used for quality improvement and problem-solving. They were developed by Kaoru Ishikawa and are applicable across industries for identifying, analyzing, and resolving quality issues.

🔹 Tool 1: Pareto Chart

Based on the Pareto principle (80/20 rule), this chart identifies the most significant factors in a dataset. It displays defects or causes in descending order of frequency, helping teams prioritize improvement efforts.

🔹 Tool 2: Cause-and-Effect Diagram

Also known as the Fishbone or Ishikawa diagram, this tool visually maps out the potential causes of a problem. It categorizes causes into major groups such as Materials, Methods, Machines, Manpower, Measurement, and Environment (6Ms).

🔹 Tools 3-7

  • Check Sheet – Simple tally sheet for collecting and organizing data
  • Histogram – Bar chart showing frequency distribution
  • Control Chart – Monitors process variation over time
  • Scatter Diagram – Shows relationship between two variables
  • Flowchart – Visual representation of process steps and decision points
