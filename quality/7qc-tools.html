🛠️ 7 QC Tools

The seven basic quality control tools used to analyze data, identify root causes, and drive process improvement.

🔹 What are the 7 QC Tools?

The 7 QC Tools are a set of simple, graphical techniques introduced by Kaoru Ishikawa for identifying and solving quality-related problems. They are widely used in manufacturing and engineering for root cause analysis, process monitoring, and continuous improvement.

🔹 The Seven Tools

  • Cause-and-Effect Diagram – Fishbone/Ishikawa diagram for root cause analysis
  • Check Sheet – Structured form for collecting and recording data
  • Control Chart – Tracks process performance over time against control limits
  • Histogram – Bar chart showing the distribution of data
  • Pareto Chart – Ranks causes by frequency to focus on the vital few
  • Scatter Diagram – Shows the relationship between two variables
  • Stratification / Flowchart – Separates data by category or maps the process flow

🔹 Why They Matter

These tools require no advanced statistical training, making them accessible to operators and engineers alike. Together they cover roughly 95% of quality-related problems encountered on the shop floor, and they form the foundation for more advanced methodologies such as Six Sigma and SPC.

🔹 Example: Reducing Defects on an Assembly Line

A team collects defect data using a check sheet, ranks defect types with a Pareto chart to find the top contributors, then uses a fishbone diagram to brainstorm root causes for the leading defect. A control chart is then used to confirm the process has stabilized after corrective actions are implemented.
