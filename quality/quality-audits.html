📊 Quality Audits

Understanding quality audits, types, processes, and best practices for internal and external auditing in quality management.

🔹 What is a Quality Audit?

A quality audit is a systematic, independent examination of a quality system or process to determine whether activities and results comply with planned arrangements, standards, and requirements. Audits identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement.

🔹 Types of Quality Audits

  • Internal Audit – Conducted by the organization itself to assess its own QMS
  • External Audit – Conducted by external bodies (certification bodies, customers, regulators)
  • First-Party Audit – Self-assessment (internal)
  • Second-Party Audit – Customer audits of suppliers
  • Third-Party Audit – Independent certification audits (e.g., ISO 9001 certification)

🔹 Audit Process Steps

  • Planning – Define scope, objectives, criteria, and audit team
  • Document Review – Review QMS documentation before the audit
  • On-Site Audit – Interviews, observations, and evidence collection
  • Audit Report – Document findings, non-conformities, and observations
  • Corrective Actions – Address non-conformities and implement improvements
  • Follow-up – Verify effectiveness of corrective actions

🔹 Example: ISO 9001 Internal Audit

A manufacturing company conducts an internal audit of its QMS to prepare for ISO 9001 recertification. The audit team reviews processes, interviews employees, and checks records. They identify a minor non-conformity in calibration records and recommend corrective action. The audit report is reviewed by management, and corrective action is implemented within 30 days.
