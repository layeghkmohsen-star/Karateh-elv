📄 Quality Documentation

Manuals, records, and certificates that provide traceability and evidence of conformance throughout the quality management system.

🔹 Why Quality Documentation Matters

Quality documentation provides objective evidence that products, processes, and systems meet specified requirements. It supports traceability, enables audits, and is a core requirement of quality management standards such as ISO 9001.

🔹 Key Document Types

  • Quality Manual – Describes the scope and structure of the quality management system
  • Procedures & Work Instructions – Step-by-step guidance for performing tasks consistently
  • Inspection & Test Records – Evidence that inspections and tests were performed and results obtained
  • Certificates of Conformance – Formal declarations that products meet specified requirements
  • Calibration Records – Traceable evidence that measuring equipment is accurate
  • Non-Conformance & Corrective Action Reports – Documentation of deviations and their resolution

🔹 Document Control Principles

Effective document control ensures the correct version of a document is available at the point of use, obsolete documents are removed or clearly marked, and changes are reviewed and approved before release. Records must be legible, readily retrievable, and retained for a defined period.

🔹 Example: Documentation Package for Project Handover

At project completion, a quality documentation package typically includes inspection and test reports, material certificates, calibration records for test equipment used, as-built drawings, and a final certificate of conformance — all compiled to support handover to the client and future maintenance activities.
