🔬 Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

Inspection methods that evaluate materials, components, and welds without causing damage, ensuring integrity and safety.

🔹 What is NDT?

Non-Destructive Testing is a group of inspection techniques used to evaluate the properties, integrity, and quality of a material, component, or weld without permanently altering it. NDT is essential for detecting surface and internal defects in critical equipment, piping, and structures.

🔹 Common NDT Methods

  • Visual Testing (VT) – Direct or remote visual examination of surfaces
  • Radiographic Testing (RT) – X-ray or gamma-ray imaging of internal structure
  • Ultrasonic Testing (UT) – High-frequency sound waves detect internal flaws
  • Magnetic Particle Testing (MT) – Detects surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials
  • Liquid Penetrant Testing (PT) – Reveals surface-breaking defects using dye penetrants
  • Eddy Current Testing (ET) – Detects surface defects and measures conductivity in conductive materials

🔹 Where NDT is Applied

NDT is widely used in welding inspection, pressure vessel and piping integrity checks, structural steel verification, pipeline inspection, and in-service equipment monitoring. Certification bodies such as ASNT provide standardized qualification levels (Level I, II, III) for NDT technicians.

🔹 Example: Weld Inspection on a Pressure Vessel

Before a pressure vessel is put into service, welds are inspected using radiographic testing to check for internal porosity or cracks, followed by ultrasonic testing to verify weld thickness and detect any subsurface flaws that radiography may have missed. Results are compared against acceptance criteria in the relevant code, such as ASME Section V.
