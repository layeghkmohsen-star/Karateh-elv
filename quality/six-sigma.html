⚙️ Six Sigma

Understanding Six Sigma methodology, DMAIC framework, tools, belts, and benefits for process improvement and quality excellence.

🔹 What is Six Sigma?

Six Sigma is a data-driven methodology for process improvement that aims to reduce defects and variability to near-zero levels. It seeks to achieve 3.4 defects per million opportunities (DPMO) by focusing on customer requirements, process capability, and statistical analysis. Developed by Motorola and widely adopted by organizations worldwide.

🔹 DMAIC Framework

  • Define – Identify the problem, project goals, and customer requirements
  • Measure – Collect data to understand current process performance
  • Analyze – Identify root causes of defects and variation
  • Improve – Develop and implement solutions to address root causes
  • Control – Sustain improvements and monitor process stability

🔹 Six Sigma Roles and Belts

  • Yellow Belt – Basic awareness, supports project teams
  • Green Belt – Leads small projects, works part-time on improvement
  • Black Belt – Leads complex projects, full-time improvement role
  • Master Black Belt – Coaches and mentors, drives strategic deployment
  • Champion – Executive sponsor, provides resources and strategic direction

🔹 Example: Six Sigma in Manufacturing

A manufacturing company uses Six Sigma to reduce defects in an assembly process. The team defines the problem (high rework rate), measures defect data, analyzes root causes (misalignment of components), improves the process by adding a fixturing system, and controls with ongoing monitoring using control charts. Result: defects reduced by 70%.
