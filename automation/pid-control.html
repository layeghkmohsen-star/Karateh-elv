🎯 PID Control

Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) control theory, tuning methods, and practical applications in industrial automation.

🔹 What is PID Control?

PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) control is a feedback control loop mechanism widely used in industrial control systems. It continuously calculates an error value as the difference between a measured process variable and a desired setpoint, and applies a correction based on proportional, integral, and derivative terms.

🔹 PID Terms

  • Proportional (P) – Responds to current error, provides fast response
  • Integral (I) – Eliminates steady-state error, accumulates past errors
  • Derivative (D) – Predicts future error, dampens overshoot

🔹 Tuning Methods

  • Ziegler-Nichols – Classic open/closed-loop tuning
  • Cohen-Coon – For process with dead time
  • Lambda Tuning – Robust and smooth response
  • Software-based – Auto-tuning in PLCs and DCS

🔹 Example: Temperature Control Loop

A PID controller maintains the temperature of a chemical reactor at 150°C. The controller receives a temperature reading from a thermocouple, compares it with the setpoint, and adjusts the steam valve position to keep the temperature stable.
