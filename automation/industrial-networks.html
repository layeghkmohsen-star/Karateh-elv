🌐 Industrial Networks

Communication protocols and network topologies used in industrial automation, including Profinet, Modbus, Ethernet/IP, and more.

🔹 What are Industrial Networks?

Industrial networks are communication systems that connect field devices, controllers, and SCADA systems in industrial environments. They must be robust, deterministic, and support real-time data exchange.

🔹 Common Protocols

  • Profinet – Industrial Ethernet for automation (Siemens)
  • Modbus RTU/TCP – Simple, widely used serial/ethernet protocol
  • Ethernet/IP – CIP-based industrial ethernet (Rockwell)
  • PROFIBUS – Fieldbus standard for process and factory automation
  • DeviceNet – CAN-based network for low-level devices

🔹 Network Topologies

  • Star – Central switch, easy troubleshooting
  • Ring – Redundant, high availability
  • Line/Daisy-chain – Simple, cost-effective
  • Tree – Hierarchical, scalable

🔹 Example: Profinet System Setup

A Profinet network consists of an IO Controller (e.g., PLC), IO Devices (remote I/O, drives), and a network switch. Configuration is done via TIA Portal or other engineering software.
