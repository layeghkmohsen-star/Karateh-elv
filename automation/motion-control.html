⚙️ Motion Control

Motion control systems including servo drives, stepper motors, positioning, and motion controllers for industrial automation.

🔹 What is Motion Control?

Motion control is a sub-field of automation that involves controlling the movement of mechanical components. It includes servo systems, stepper motors, positioning, and coordinated multi-axis motion for applications like robotics, CNC, and packaging machinery.

🔹 Key Components

  • Motion Controller – PLC or dedicated motion controller
  • Servo Drive – Amplifier for servo motors
  • Servo Motor – High-performance motor with feedback
  • Stepper Motor – Open-loop positioning motor
  • Encoder/Resolver – Position feedback device

🔹 Motion Control Applications

  • Robotics – Multi-axis coordinated motion
  • CNC Machines – Precision machining
  • Packaging – High-speed indexing
  • Conveyor Systems – Variable speed control
  • Printing – Synchronized web control

🔹 Example: Pick-and-Place Robot

A servo-driven pick-and-place robot uses coordinated motion control to move products from one conveyor to another. The motion controller ensures precise timing and smooth acceleration/deceleration for high-speed operation.
