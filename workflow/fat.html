🧪 Factory Acceptance Test (FAT)

The Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) is a critical quality milestone performed at the supplier's facility to verify that equipment meets specifications before shipping to site.

🔹 What is FAT?

Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) is the process of testing equipment and systems at the manufacturer's factory before they are shipped to the project site. It validates that the equipment performs according to specifications and meets all functional and performance requirements. FAT reduces the risk of failures during installation and commissioning.

🔹 Key FAT Activities

  • Visual Inspection – Check for physical defects, labeling, and correct component installation
  • Functional Testing – Test all control functions, interlocks, and sequences
  • Performance Testing – Verify capacity, efficiency, and response times
  • Safety Testing – Ensure all safety systems and interlocks operate correctly
  • Documentation Review – Verify all manuals, drawings, and certificates are complete
  • Test Reports – Document all test results and sign off

🔹 FAT Participants

  • Supplier – Manufacturer or fabricator of the equipment
  • Client – Owner or project representative
  • Engineering Team – Design engineers who verify compliance with specifications
  • Quality Team – QA/QC personnel ensuring quality standards are met
  • Third-Party Inspectors – Independent inspection agencies (if required)

🔹 Example: FAT for a Motor Control Center

A motor control center (MCC) is subjected to FAT at the supplier's workshop. The tests include verifying wiring against schematics, testing each starter for proper operation, checking interlock logic, and verifying communication to the SCADA system. A punch list is created for any deficiencies, which are corrected before shipment. The client signs off after all tests pass.
