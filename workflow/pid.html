📄 P&ID (Piping & Instrumentation Diagram)

P&ID is a detailed graphical representation of the process flow, equipment, piping, instrumentation, and control systems in an industrial plant.

🔹 What is a P&ID?

A Piping and Instrumentation Diagram (P&ID) is a schematic drawing that shows the relationships between piping, instrumentation, and control systems in a process plant. It is a fundamental engineering document used for design, construction, and operation of industrial facilities.

🔹 Key Elements of a P&ID

  • Piping – Lines, fittings, valves, and flow directions
  • Instrumentation – Sensors, transmitters, controllers, and indicators
  • Equipment – Vessels, pumps, compressors, heat exchangers, and reactors
  • Control Systems – Control loops, interlocks, and logic
  • Process Flow – Material and energy flow through the system

🔹 P&ID Standards

  • ISA 5.1 – Instrumentation Symbols and Identification
  • ANSI/ISA 5.1 – Graphical symbols for instrumentation
  • ISO 10628 – Diagrams for chemical and petrochemical industry
  • IEC 60617 – Graphical symbols for electrical diagrams

🔹 Example: P&ID for a Chemical Reactor

A P&ID for a chemical reactor shows the reactor vessel, feed lines, product outlet, cooling water system, temperature and pressure sensors, and control valves. The diagram includes flow indicators, temperature controllers, and safety interlocks. It serves as the basis for design, hazard analysis, and operating procedures.
