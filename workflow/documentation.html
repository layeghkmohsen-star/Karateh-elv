📝 Documentation

Documentation is the final phase of the engineering workflow where all project documents, as-built drawings, manuals, and records are compiled and handed over to the client for operations and maintenance.

🔹 What is Documentation?

Documentation is the process of compiling, organizing, and delivering all project-related documents to the client. It includes as-built drawings, operation and maintenance manuals, test reports, certificates, and other records necessary for the safe and efficient operation of the facility. Proper documentation ensures that the client has all the information needed to operate, maintain, and troubleshoot the system.

🔹 Key Documentation Deliverables

  • As-Built Drawings – Updated drawings reflecting the actual installation
  • Operation and Maintenance Manuals – Comprehensive guides for operators and maintenance personnel
  • Test Reports – Records of all FAT, SAT, and commissioning tests
  • Equipment Datasheets – Technical specifications of all installed equipment
  • Certificates – Calibration certificates, material test certificates, and safety certificates
  • Warranty Documents – Manufacturer warranties and service agreements
  • Spare Parts Lists – Recommended spare parts for maintenance

🔹 Documentation Standards

  • ISO 9001 – Quality Management Systems (document control)
  • ISO 10006 – Quality Management for Projects
  • IEC 61355 – Classification of Technical Documentation
  • API 551 – Documentation for Process Measurement

🔹 Example: Documentation Handover for a Power Plant

A power plant project is completed and ready for handover. The documentation team compiles all as-built drawings (electrical, mechanical, civil), operation manuals (start-up, normal operation, shutdown, emergency procedures), maintenance manuals (preventive, corrective), test reports (FAT, SAT, commissioning), and certificates. All documents are organized in a digital document management system and physical binders. The client reviews and accepts the documentation package before final handover.
