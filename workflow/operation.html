⚙️ Operation

Operation is the final stage of the engineering workflow where the facility is handed over to the client and begins normal production or service, with ongoing monitoring and maintenance.

🔹 What is Operation?

Operation is the phase where the completed facility or system is put into normal service after successful commissioning and handover. It involves running the plant or system to produce the intended output (e.g., power, products, services) while maintaining safety, efficiency, and reliability. Operation includes monitoring, maintenance, troubleshooting, and continuous improvement to ensure optimal performance.

🔹 Key Operation Activities

  • Monitoring & Control – Operating the system from the control room, monitoring process variables, and adjusting parameters as needed
  • Preventive Maintenance – Regular inspections, servicing, and component replacements to prevent failures
  • Corrective Maintenance – Repairing or replacing failed components to restore normal operation
  • Performance Optimization – Continuously improving efficiency, output, and quality
  • Troubleshooting – Diagnosing and resolving issues that arise during operation
  • Safety Management – Enforcing safety procedures and responding to emergencies
  • Data Analysis – Analyzing operational data to identify trends and opportunities for improvement

🔹 Operation Documentation

  • Shift Logs – Records of daily operations, events, and deviations
  • Maintenance Records – History of maintenance activities and repairs
  • Operating Procedures – Standard procedures for normal, abnormal, and emergency conditions
  • Performance Reports – Reports on production, efficiency, and quality
  • Permits and Clearances – Work permits for maintenance and modifications

🔹 Example: Operation of a Manufacturing Plant

A manufacturing plant begins normal production after commissioning. The operations team monitors the production line from the control room, ensuring that throughput, quality, and safety targets are met. Maintenance teams perform weekly inspections, quarterly servicing, and handle breakdown repairs. Continuous improvement projects aim to reduce downtime, increase output, and lower energy consumption. The plant operates 24/7 with a three-shift system.
