✏️ Engineering Design Phase

The engineering design phase translates process requirements into detailed technical specifications, drawings, and plans for construction and fabrication.

🔹 What is the Design Phase?

The design phase is where the process requirements and P&ID are converted into detailed engineering documents. This includes schematics, panel layouts, cable routing, earthing designs, load calculations, and equipment specifications. It bridges the gap between process design and physical construction.

🔹 Key Activities in the Design Phase

  • Electrical Schematics – Single-line diagrams, wiring diagrams
  • Panel Design – Layout, component selection, enclosure sizing
  • Cable Routing – Cable trays, conduits, wireways
  • Earthing & Grounding – Design for safety and protection
  • Load Calculation – Power requirements and distribution
  • Instrumentation – Loop diagrams, instrument schedules

🔹 Design Deliverables

  • Detailed engineering drawings (CAD)
  • Equipment datasheets and specifications
  • Material take-off (MTO) and bill of materials (BOM)
  • Installation and testing procedures
  • Operation and maintenance manuals

🔹 Example: Design of a Motor Control Center

An MCC (Motor Control Center) is designed for a new production line. The design includes incoming power distribution, feeder breakers, contactors, overload relays, and PLC interface. All components are selected based on motor ratings and environmental conditions. The design package includes schematics, panel layout, and cable schedules.
