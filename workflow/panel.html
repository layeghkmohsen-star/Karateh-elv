📦 Panel Design

Design and layout of electrical control panels, including component selection, enclosure sizing, wiring, and compliance with standards.

🔹 What is Panel Design?

Panel design is the process of planning and creating electrical control panels that house circuit breakers, contactors, relays, PLCs, and other components. It involves selecting the right enclosure size, arranging components for optimal airflow and maintenance, and ensuring compliance with safety and performance standards.

🔹 Key Components of a Control Panel

  • Enclosure – Cabinet with IP rating (IP54, IP66) for protection against dust and moisture
  • Main Switch – Isolates the panel from power supply
  • Circuit Breakers – Protection for incoming and outgoing circuits
  • Contactors & Relays – Switching and control of loads
  • PLCs/Controllers – The brain of the automation
  • Terminal Blocks – For organizing and terminating wiring
  • Power Supplies – Convert AC to DC for control circuits

🔹 Panel Design Standards

  • IEC 61439 – Low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies
  • IEC 60529 – IP Ratings (Ingress Protection)
  • UL 508A – Industrial Control Panels (US)
  • IEC 60204 – Safety of machinery – Electrical equipment
  • NEC Article 409 – Industrial Control Panels

🔹 Example: Motor Control Panel (MCP)

A motor control panel is designed for a 45 kW induction motor. The panel includes an incoming circuit breaker, contactor, overload relay, and start/stop push buttons. A PLC interface allows remote monitoring and control. The panel is rated IP54 for use in a dusty environment. The layout ensures clearances for heat dissipation and easy access for maintenance.
