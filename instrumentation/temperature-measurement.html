🌡️ Temperature Measurement

Methods and devices for measuring temperature in industrial processes: RTDs, thermocouples, thermistors, and infrared sensors.

🔹 Importance of Temperature Measurement

Temperature is one of the most critical process variables in industries such as chemical, petrochemical, food, pharmaceuticals, and power generation. Accurate measurement ensures product quality, process efficiency, and equipment safety.

🔹 Common Temperature Sensors

  • RTD (Resistance Temperature Detector) – High accuracy, wide range (-200 to 850°C). Commonly PT100.
  • Thermocouple – Rugged, wide range (-200 to 2300°C). Types: J, K, T, S, R.
  • Thermistor – High sensitivity, limited range (-50 to 300°C).
  • Infrared Pyrometer – Non-contact measurement for high temperatures.

🔹 Example: RTD PT100 for Reactor Temperature

In a chemical reactor, a 3-wire PT100 RTD is installed to measure temperature continuously. The RTD connects to a transmitter that outputs 4-20 mA to the DCS. The transmitter is calibrated to a range of 0-200°C, providing accurate control of the exothermic reaction.
