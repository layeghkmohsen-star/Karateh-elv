📘 Overview of Instrumentation Engineering

A comprehensive overview of instrumentation engineering, covering sensors, transmitters, analyzers, control valves, and measurement systems.

🔹 What is Instrumentation Engineering?

Instrumentation engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with measurement and control of process variables such as temperature, pressure, flow, level, and chemical composition. It is essential for automation and process control in industries like oil & gas, chemical, pharmaceutical, and power generation.

🔹 Key Areas

  • Sensors and Transmitters (temperature, pressure, flow, level)
  • Control Valves and Actuators
  • Analyzers (pH, conductivity, gas chromatography)
  • Calibration and Testing
  • P&ID and Loop Diagrams

🔹 Key Standards

  • ISA 5.1 – Instrumentation Symbols and Identification
  • IEC 61511 – Functional Safety for Process Industry
  • API 551 – Process Measurement
  • ANSI/ISA 88 – Batch Control
