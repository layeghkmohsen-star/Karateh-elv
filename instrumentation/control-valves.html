🔧 Control Valves

An overview of control valves, actuators, positioners, and their applications in industrial process control.

🔹 What are Control Valves?

Control valves are final control elements that regulate the flow of fluids in process systems. They modulate flow based on signals from a controller (PLC, DCS) to maintain process variables such as temperature, pressure, level, or flow at desired setpoints.

🔹 Key Components

  • Valve Body – The main housing with flow passages (globe, ball, butterfly, etc.)
  • Actuator – Provides the force to move the valve stem (pneumatic, electric, hydraulic)
  • Positioner – Ensures the valve opens to the exact signal position
  • I/P Converter – Converts 4-20 mA to pneumatic pressure
  • Limit Switches – Provide open/closed status feedback

🔹 Valve Types

  • Globe Valve – For throttling control, high pressure drop
  • Ball Valve – Quarter-turn, for on/off and throttling
  • Butterfly Valve – Quarter-turn, for large pipes
  • Gate Valve – On/off, low pressure drop
  • Control Valve (Sliding Stem) – Precision control for critical loops

🔹 Example: Globe Control Valve for Steam

A Fisher globe control valve with a pneumatic actuator and positioner is installed on a steam line to control temperature in a heat exchanger. The DCS sends a 4-20 mA signal to the I/P converter, which modulates the valve position to regulate steam flow and maintain the temperature at 150°C.
