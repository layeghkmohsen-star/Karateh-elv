🧪 Analyzers

Industrial analyzers for measuring pH, conductivity, dissolved oxygen, gas composition, and other chemical properties in process streams.

🔹 What are Analyzers?

Analyzers are instruments that measure chemical or physical properties of process fluids. They provide continuous or discrete measurements of parameters such as pH, conductivity, turbidity, gas concentration, and more. They are critical for quality control, safety, and regulatory compliance.

🔹 Common Analyzer Types

  • pH Analyzer – Measures acidity/alkalinity using a glass electrode
  • Conductivity Analyzer – Measures ionic content of water
  • Dissolved Oxygen (DO) Analyzer – Measures oxygen in water (e.g., wastewater)
  • Gas Analyzer – Measures O2, CO2, CO, NOx, etc. (e.g., infrared, paramagnetic)
  • Turbidity Analyzer – Measures suspended particles in liquid

🔹 Example: pH Analyzer in Chemical Plant

A pH analyzer with a glass electrode is installed in a chemical reactor to monitor pH during a neutralization reaction. The analyzer continuously transmits pH values to the DCS, which controls the addition of acid or base to maintain pH within the set range (6.8-7.2).
